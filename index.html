Bio

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Brazilian Center of Analysis and Planning (CEBRAP). I received a PhD in Public Administration and Government from Getulio Vargas Foundation (Brazil).

My research focuses on the relationship between politics and religion. With a particular interest in Evangelical and Catholic groups, I study political economy, electoral strategies of religious parties and candidates, and religious lobby and its influences on policies in Brazil.
